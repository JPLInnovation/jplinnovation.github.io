FRC 2026 Season

This season's challenge centers on game piece scoring. Teams design and build robots capable of collecting and scoring game pieces into designated alliance goals.

2026 FRC Official Playing Field Layout

Game Overview

Competition Season: January – April 2026

Two alliances, each consisting of three robots, compete head-to-head. The objective is to collect game pieces from the field and score them into alliance goals. Game pieces are distributed across the field, and robots may collect, control, and score them in designated scoring zones. Each alliance has multiple scoring locations with different point values. The alliance with the highest point total at the end of the match wins.

Game Pieces

Game pieces are distributed across the field. Robots intake these pieces and deliver them to scoring zones.

Scoring Zones

Each alliance has multiple scoring locations including high goals and low goals. High goals offer greater point value but require more precision.

Match Structure

Each match lasts 2 minutes and 30 seconds and consists of:

1. Autonomous Period

Duration: 15 seconds
Robots operate using pre-programmed instructions only. Driver control is not permitted. Bonus points may be awarded for successful autonomous actions.

2. Teleoperated Period

Duration: 2 minutes 15 seconds
Drivers control robots to score game pieces and complete objectives.

3. Endgame

Duration: Final 30 seconds (overlaps with teleop)
Robots may attempt endgame tasks such as climbing, docking, parking, or activating field elements for bonus points.

Scoring Breakdown

Game Piece Scoring
  • High Goal: Higher point value, requires precision
  • Low Goal: Lower point value, easier scoring option
  • Autonomous Bonus: Additional points for scoring during autonomous
Game Piece Control
  • Game pieces are available at loading stations and on the field
  • Robots may control a limited number of game pieces at once
  • Human players assist by feeding pieces at designated stations
Endgame Scoring
  • Parking: Small bonus points
  • Low Climb / Dock: Medium bonus points
  • High Climb / Traversal: Large bonus points
Ranking Points (Qualification Matches)
  • Win: 2 ranking points
  • Tie: 1 ranking point
  • Loss: 0 ranking points
  • Bonus RP: Awarded for completing specific objectives
