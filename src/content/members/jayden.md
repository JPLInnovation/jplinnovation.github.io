I’m a Vietnamese boy who grew up in Ho Chi Minh City in a middle-class family. My hometown is originally from both Quang Tri and Nghe An. I’ve always been interested in cars and aviation. My dad was my role model—he inspired my curiosity about computers. He used to buy a lot of laptops from China, and he once gave me a 16GB RAM stick and a SATA SSD. I tried finding the best used laptop to upgrade with those parts, but I didn’t realize at the time that most of those laptops were old and had weak CPUs and GPUs.

Later, I joined a LEGO Mindstorms class, where I discovered my passion for building and programming. From there, I moved on to Python and eventually to C++. At the start of Grade 9, I learned more about computer hardware—CPUs, GPUs, and how Windows boots, including things like changing storage devices.

After that, I shifted to studying infrastructure administration. I learned some CCNA-related concepts, though not very deeply at first. Toward the end of the year, I moved to a different academy (VN Pro), where I learned much more. Now, I’m able to configure routers, Layer 2 and Layer 3 switches, and understand the concepts behind them.

Currently, I am actively involved in the FIRST Robotics Competition (FRC), where I continue to apply and expand my skills in programming and engineering in a competitive environment.
